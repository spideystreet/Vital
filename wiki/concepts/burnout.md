# Burnout

## WHO ICD-11 Definition

Burn-out is classified under **ICD-11 code Z73.0** ("State of vital exhaustion"), under "Factors influencing health status or contact with health services" — not as a medical disease.

**Definition:** "Syndrome conceptualized as resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed."

### Three Dimensions (ICD-11 / MBI)
1. **Exhaustion** — depleted energy, persistent fatigue
2. **Cynicism / Depersonalization** — growing mental distance from one's job, negative feelings toward work
3. **Reduced professional efficacy** — loss of confidence in one's own performance

The gold-standard assessment tool is the **Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI)**, validated internationally.

**Important:** Burn-out "refers specifically to phenomena in the occupational context and should not be applied to describe experiences in other areas of life."

## The Physiology — How Burnout Develops

Burnout is the **endpoint of a measurable physiological degradation process**, trackable weeks or months before clinical burnout appears.

### Two Stress Axes

**1. HPA Axis (Hormonal)** — Hypothalamus → Pituitary → Adrenal cortex → **Cortisol**
- Chronic stress → HPA dysregulation → elevated or blunted Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR)
- Burnout patients show abnormal CAR — both hyper and hypo secretion are pathological
- Hair cortisol confirmed as most reliable chronic stress biomarker (ICA Health systematic review, 2022)

**2. SAM Axis (Neurological)** — Sympathetic fight-or-flight
- Increases: heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate
- **Decreases: HRV** (the primary measurable signal via wearables)
- Sympathetic overload persists even at rest under chronic stress

### The Burnout Cascade

```
Chronic work stressor
        │
        ▼
Repeated HPA + SAM activation
        │
        ▼
Cortisol dysregulation + sympathetic overload
        │
        ├──► Sleep disruption (reduced deep sleep, fragmented recovery)
        ├──► Immune suppression (inflammatory markers rise)
        ├──► HRV suppression (reduced parasympathetic recovery)
        └──► Progressive exhaustion → Cynicism → Efficacy loss
                        │
                        ▼
                   BURNOUT (ICD-11 Z73.0)
```

### Sleep as the Central Mediator

- Elevated cortisol and sympathetic activity **reduce sleep quality** directly
- Chronic insomnia linked to **24-hour hypersecretion of ACTH and cortisol** — feedback loop
- **Reduced deep sleep reduces HRV recovery**, further suppressing parasympathetic system
- Bayesian modeling (2025): excessive daily stress reduces deep sleep, physical activity increases it
- Nocturnal HRV + sleep stages = the **two strongest wearable-based predictors** of burnout

> *Sources: Frontiers in Psychology (2025), Semantic Scholar narrative review (2025), ICA Health (2022)*

### Target Segments

- **Managers & Cadres:** 52% high burnout risk (HBR 2022). Higher Apple Watch penetration.
- **Healthcare workers:** Highest prevalence sector. 40% severe burnout in ENT surgeons (STROBE, 2024).
- **Gen Z (< 29 ans):** 59% report burnout symptoms. Digital-native, highest distress levels.

## Economic Impact

- **INRS study (2007):** Cost of work-related stress in France = **€2–3 billion/year**
- **Burnout absence:** Average 112 days per employee, replacement cost 6–9 months of salary
- **500-employee company at 12% risk:** ~60 employees × €30K–50K cost = **€1.8M–3M annual at-risk cost**
- **Prevention ROI:** Tool at €15–30/employee/month pays for itself at 1–2 avoided cases/year

## Risk Factors (French Gov)

- Workload and time pressure, lack of autonomy, poor recognition, quality of management, work–life balance issues
- **Legal:** Employers have **legal obligation to prevent psychosocial risks** under French labour law
- **PST 4 (Plan Santé au Travail 2021–2025):** targets psychosocial risk prevention, mental health monitoring, digital tools for occupational health
