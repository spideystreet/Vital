# France Market Opportunity

## The Scale of the Problem

France has a **documented burnout epidemic**, among the most affected European countries (Forum of Future, 2023).

|---|---|---|

## WHO Recognition & Regulatory Context

WHO added burnout to ICD-11 (code **Z73.0**) in 2022 — legitimizes as medical issue, opens door for health insurance coverage, creates regulatory clarity for wellness/prevention apps (avoiding CE marking/MDR at MVP stage).

**PST 4 (Plan Santé au Travail 2021–2025)** explicitly targets:
- Psychosocial risk prevention (RPS)
- Mental health monitoring in workplace
- Digital tools for occupational health

## Economic Argument for B2B

- Average burnout absence: **112 days per employee**
- Replacement cost: **6–9 months of salary**
- 500-employee company at 12% risk: **~60 employees × €30K–50K = €1.8M–3M annual at-risk cost**
- Prevention at €15–30/employee/month pays for itself at 1–2 avoided cases/year
- ROI measurable, tied to HR absenteeism metrics, aligns with PST 4 obligations

## Data Privacy

- **RGPD (GDPR)** — consent-based, data minimization
- **Référentiel HDS** — required for health data storage
- **CNIL guidelines** on biometric/employee monitoring
- V.I.T.A.L positioning: user owns HealthKit data, no raw biometrics on servers, aggregated trend scores only, wellness tool (not medical device at MVP)
