## WHO Recognition & Regulatory Context

In 2022, the WHO officially added burnout to the ICD-11 under code **Z73.0** ("State of vital exhaustion"), recognizing it as an occupational phenomenon. This:

- Legitimizes burnout as a **medical issue** requiring intervention
- Opens the door for **health insurance coverage** of prevention tools
- Creates regulatory clarity for apps positioned as **wellness + prevention** (not diagnostic)

In France, the **Plan Santé au Travail (PST 4, 2021–2025)** by the Ministère du Travail explicitly targets:
- Psychosocial risk prevention (RPS)
- Mental health monitoring in the workplace
- Digital tools for occupational health

## The Economic Argument for Enterprise Sales (B2B)

For the Alan × Mistral hackathon and beyond, the B2B pitch is the strongest entry point:

- Average burnout absence: **112 days per employee**
- Average replacement cost: **6–9 months of salary**
- For a company of 500 employees at 12% burnout risk: **~60 employees at risk × €30,000–50,000 cost** = **€1.8M–3M annual at-risk cost**
- A prevention tool at €15–30/employee/month pays for itself at 1–2 avoided burnout cases per year

The ROI is measurable, directly tied to HR absenteeism metrics, and aligns with France's PST 4 obligations.

## Data Privacy Framework

In France, health data is governed by:
- **RGPD (GDPR)** — consent-based, data minimization required
- **Référentiel HDS (Hébergement Données de Santé)** — required for storing medical/health data
- **CNIL guidelines** on biometric data and employee monitoring

**For V.I.T.A.L**, the safest positioning is:
- User owns their HealthKit data — app reads, never stores raw biometrics on servers
- Aggregated, anonymized trend scores stored (not raw HRV or sleep data)
- Explicit RGPD consent flow at onboarding
- Position as **wellness tool**, not medical device (avoids CE marking / MDR requirements at MVP stage)
